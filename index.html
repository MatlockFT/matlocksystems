A Revised Scoring System

This system revolves around three main criteria: Damage, Control, and Defense, scored from 0 to 3 points per round. Each criterion is weighted differently, with the total score for a round ranging from 0 to 9 points.

1. Damage (0-3 points) — 50% Weight

Damage is the most important factor. You assess how much harm a fighter inflicts, focusing on strikes, submissions, and actions that change the course of the fight. Here's what to look for:

  • Striking Impact: The power and severity of strikes, whether punches, kicks, knees, or elbows. Also consider visual damage like cuts or swelling, knockdowns, or hits that change momentum.
  • Submission Threat: How close a submission attempt comes to finishing the fight or forcing significant defense.

Scoring Damage:

  • 3 Points: Fight changing damage like knockdowns, severe cuts, or near-finishes from submissions.
  • 2 Points: Noticeable damage, like swelling or strong but defended submission attempts.
  • 1 Point: Minor damage, such as light strikes or submission attempts with minimal threat.
  • 0 Points: No effective damage caused.

2. Control (0-3 points) — 30% Weight

Control covers a fighter’s ability to steer the fight’s flow, including maintaining dominant positions and pressuring the opponent.

  • Positional Dominance: Holding positions like top control, mount, or back control. Keeping the opponent from escaping or countering is key.
  • Aggression and Cage Control: Moving forward, cutting off the cage, and forcing the opponent to react defensively.

Scoring Control:

  • 3 Points: Full control of positions or constant pressure without any significant escape from the opponent.
  • 2 Points: Mostly controlling the fight with occasional resistance.
  • 1 Point: Brief moments of control, but unable to maintain dominance.
  • 0 Points: No control or ability to dictate the fight’s flow.

3. Defense (0-3 points) — 20% Weight

Defense is about preventing damage and neutralizing the opponent’s offense. This includes avoiding strikes, submissions, and escaping bad positions.

  • Striking Defense: Effectively blocking or dodging attacks with minimal damage taken.
  • Grappling Defense: Stopping takedowns, reversing positions, and defending submissions. Escaping from dangerous spots also counts here.

Scoring Defense:

  • 3 Points: Completely neutralizes the opponent’s offense, turning defense into offense.
  • 2 Points: Avoids most damage and stays out of danger but doesn’t fully shut down the opponent.
  • 1 Point: Defends well but still takes some damage or gets caught in tough positions.
  • 0 Points: Poor defense, takes substantial damage or frequently ends up in submissions or bad spots.

Total Round Score Calculation

To calculate the score for a round, add up the points for each criterion and apply the weighted formula:

Total Round Score = (Damage × 0.5) + (Control × 0.3) + (Defense × 0.2)

The maximum score for a round is 9 points, while the minimum is 0.

At the end of the fight, the total score across all rounds determines the winner.
